# Don't work ungrounded — a goal first, a plan for anything multi-step

Before doing substantive work, know what you're aiming at. Building without a clear goal produces
motion, not progress — plausible output that may solve the wrong thing. When the target isn't
clear, **stop and establish it first** instead of guessing.

- **A goal is always required.** Before writing, building, or changing anything non-trivial, there
  must be a clear statement of what "done" looks like and why it matters. If it's missing, surface
  that and get it set — don't infer a goal from thin context and run with it.
- **A plan is required once the work is multi-step.** For anything beyond a small, self-contained
  change, there should be a rough roadmap — the steps and their order — before execution starts. A
  one-line fix needs a goal, not a roadmap; a feature needs both.
- **Don't silently fill the gap.** If the goal or the plan is missing, say so plainly and ask —
  don't invent one and proceed as though it were given.
- **Trivial and exploratory work is exempt.** Quick questions, reads, and genuine exploration to
  *discover* the goal don't need one up front — that's how you find it. The gate is for committing
  to substantive, hard-to-reverse work.
